# Game Record

# Dota 2

## Summary
- **Date of Birth:** July 9, 2013
- **Engine:** Source 2
- **Genre:** Multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA)
- **Composer:** Tim Larkin

## Release
Dota 2 was announced by Valve in 2010 and entered a closed beta phase in 2011. The game was officially released as a free-to-play title on Steam on July 9, 2013. Dota 2 is a sequel to the original Defense of the Ancients, which was a community-created mod for Warcraft III. In 2015, the game was ported from the original Source engine to Source 2, making it the first game to use the updated engine. The transition brought improved performance, a new dashboard, and custom game support.

## Monetization
Dota 2 is entirely free-to-play with no gameplay advantages available for purchase. All heroes are unlocked from the start. Revenue comes from cosmetic items such as hero skins, couriers, wards, and terrain packs. Players can purchase items through the in-game store, the Steam Community Market, or through seasonal Battle Passes. The Battle Pass, typically released around The International tournament, offers exclusive cosmetic rewards through a tiered progression system.

## Revenue
Dota 2 has generated billions of dollars in revenue primarily through cosmetic microtransactions and Battle Pass sales. The International prize pools, which are crowdfunded through Battle Pass purchases, have exceeded $40 million in a single year, with The International 2021 reaching a record prize pool of over $40 million.
